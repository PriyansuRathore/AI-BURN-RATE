# ECONOMICS

This file still needs a full spreadsheet-style pass with explicit assumptions and math before submission.

A reasonable early model is to treat completed audits as the top of the funnel, consultations as the key middle step, and actual credit purchases as the monetization event. The lead value is driven by the subset of users with meaningful monthly savings opportunities, especially teams paying for multiple premium AI seats or direct API usage without negotiated pricing advantages. If Credex earns margin on discounted credits, then the useful question is how often a completed audit identifies savings large enough to justify a conversation and how often those conversations convert into actual credit movement.

The main economic challenge is that many users will discover they are only overspending by a small amount. That is not bad for the product, but it lowers direct monetization efficiency unless the tool is cheap to distribute and efficient at identifying high-intent, high-savings accounts. In that sense, the economics likely work only if the tool behaves less like a consumer utility and more like a precision lead filter for companies whose AI spend is already material.
