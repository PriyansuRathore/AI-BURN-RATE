# METRICS

The most useful North Star metric for this product is likely **qualified savings-positive audits per week**. A raw visit count is too shallow, and generic DAU does not fit a tool that many users may only need occasionally. What matters is whether the app is identifying real overspend cases that are strong enough to justify follow-up.

Three input metrics that likely drive that North Star:

- audit completion rate from landing page visit
- percentage of completed audits with estimated savings above a meaningful threshold
- percentage of savings-positive audits that convert into lead capture

The first instrumentation I would add is a funnel from landing-page visit to audit start, audit complete, share-link generated, lead captured, and consultation-intent click. That would show whether the product problem is acquisition, form friction, weak recommendation quality, or poor CTA placement.

A likely pivot trigger would be a high audit completion rate but very low rate of savings-positive outcomes. That would suggest either the target audience is wrong or the product is not reaching companies with enough spend pain to make Credex relevant.
